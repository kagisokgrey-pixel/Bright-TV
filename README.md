# Bright-Tv
Overview
BrightTV, a subscription-based television provider, wanted to grow its subscriber base and asked for insights to support the Customer Value Management (CVM) team's strategy. I was given two raw datasets : a user profile table (demographics: gender, race, age, province) and a viewership/session table (10,000 viewing records across 18 channels, spanning January–March 2016)  and tasked with uncovering who watches, when they watch, what drives consumption, and what BrightTV could do to grow and re-engage its audience.

Process
I started by mapping out an analytical framework in Miro, breaking the problem into the business objective, the key metrics that mattered (consumption, sessions, user growth), and the specific questions the data needed to answer (who are the users, when do they watch, what do they watch, how long, and who is genuinely engaged). I planned the project timeline with a Gantt chart in Canva, covering setup, cleaning, EDA, deep-dive analysis, insight generation, and presentation build across four weeks.
For data processing, I used Databricks (SQL/PySpark) to clean and prepare the raw data — handling blank entries in gender, race, and province, resolving duplicate channel names, converting session timestamps from UTC to South African time, joining the user profile table to the viewership table on UserID, and engineering new fields such as hour of day, day of week, session duration in minutes, and age-group buckets. I then used Excel to build pivot tables and supporting charts for a deeper cut of the cleaned data, and built out interactive dashboards in Power BI, Google Looker Studio, and a live web dashboard hosted via Lovable AI to visualize channel performance, viewer behaviour, and audience segments. Findings were pulled together into a final stakeholder presentation.

What I found
Viewership grew steadily over the quarter, climbing from 2,203 records in January to 4,821 in March — a 119% increase, with March alone accounting for nearly half of all activity in the dataset. Sport content dominated consumption: Supersport Live Events and the ICC Cricket World Cup 2011 broadcast together drove roughly 31% of all viewing, with the World Cup broadcast also holding attention longest at close to 17 minutes per session. Viewing peaked in the afternoon (3pm) with mornings a close second, weekday viewing outweighed weekend viewing more than 2:1, and Saturday was the single busiest day. Gauteng led all regions, and Young Adults were the largest audience segment. The data also surfaced two data-quality flags worth addressing: the recorded audience skewed heavily male (90/10), and marketing opt-in fields were uniformly set to "yes" across every record, making them unusable for targeting until corrected.

Tools used
Databricks (SQL) for data cleaning and transformation, Excel for pivot tables and analysis, Power BI, Google Looker Studio, and Lovable AI for dashboarding, Miro for project planning, Canva for the Gantt chart, and PowerPoint for the final stakeholder presentation.
